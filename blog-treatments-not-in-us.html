5 Skin Treatments You Can't Get in the US — But Can in Korea

May 2026  ·  6 min read

If you've been researching Korean skin care, you've probably noticed the same names keep appearing: Rejuran Healer, Juvelook, exosome therapy. These aren't trends. They're clinically established treatments performed daily at Seoul's top dermatology clinics — and they're essentially unavailable in the United States. Not because American doctors don't know about them, but because they haven't received FDA approval. For North American women who've hit a ceiling with what their local med spa can offer, it's one of the most compelling reasons to consider a Korea beauty trip.

1. Rejuran Healer (PDRN Injection)

Not FDA-Approved

What it is

Rejuran is a polynucleotide (PDRN) injection derived from salmon DNA that works by activating your skin's own repair mechanisms. Unlike fillers that add volume, Rejuran regenerates the skin itself — improving texture, elasticity, fine lines, and hydration from within. It's considered a foundational treatment in Korean dermatology, often described as "skin rehabilitation" rather than cosmetic correction.

Why you can't get it in the US

PDRN-based injectables have not received FDA approval for use in the United States, meaning no US med spa or dermatologist can legally administer them. In Korea, Rejuran has been widely used since 2014 with an extensive clinical track record. Many Korean women have it done annually as preventative maintenance — the equivalent of getting a dental clean.

What to expect

Most patients see meaningful improvement in skin quality, refined texture, and reduced fine lines after 2–3 sessions spaced 3–4 weeks apart. Results are gradual and cumulative rather than immediate.

2. Juvelook — Next-Generation Skin Booster

Not FDA-Approved

What it is

Juvelook is a Korean-developed injectable that combines hyaluronic acid with poly-D,L-lactic acid (PDLA) microspheres. The HA provides immediate hydration while the PDLA microspheres dissolve slowly over months, continuously stimulating new collagen. Most standard fillers do one or the other — Juvelook does both simultaneously, making it particularly effective for overall skin quality rather than targeted volume correction.

Why you can't get it in the US

PDLA microsphere formulations in injectable form have not been approved by the FDA. Juvelook is a Korean-developed product widely used across Asia, but it hasn't entered the North American market and there's no clear timeline for when it will.

What to expect

Plumper, more hydrated skin immediately, followed by progressive collagen stimulation over 3–6 months. Often described as restructuring the skin's foundation rather than topping it up.

3. Exosome Therapy

Not FDA-Approved

What it is

Exosomes are nano-sized particles derived from stem cells that carry growth factors and signaling molecules. Applied topically immediately after microneedling, RF, or laser treatments, they dramatically accelerate healing, reduce redness, and amplify results. The combination with RF microneedling or fractional laser is particularly potent — it's not so much a standalone treatment as a powerful amplifier for other procedures.

Why you can't get it in the US

In 2019, the FDA issued warnings against several exosome-based products, and reputable clinical-grade exosome treatments have remained largely inaccessible in the US since. Korean clinics use rigorously tested formulations that meet Korean Ministry of Food and Drug Safety standards — the regulatory environment is simply different.

What to expect

Faster healing post-treatment, reduced inflammation, and enhanced regeneration. Most often used as an add-on to maximize the results of another procedure done on the same day.

4. Oligio — Korean RF Tightening

Not FDA-Cleared in US

What it is

Oligio is a Korean monopolar radiofrequency device designed for skin tightening and lifting. It delivers RF energy to multiple skin layers simultaneously, producing firming and lifting results comparable to HIFU — but with notably less discomfort and downtime. It's particularly effective around the jawline, jowls, and cheeks where mild laxity is developing.

Why you can't get it in the US

Oligio is a Korean-developed device that has not received FDA clearance for use in the United States. US providers have access to similar devices (Thermage, Profound RF), but Oligio specifically is not among them.

What to expect

Skin tightening and improved contour that builds progressively over 3–6 months as new collagen forms. Typically one session is sufficient to see clear results, though some patients return for a follow-up at 6 months.
